# Strategy & Finance Expertise Profile

## Overview

The Strategy & Finance specialist is a product manager, strategist, and financial analyst with deep expertise in product strategy, roadmap planning, financial modeling, business case development, OKR frameworks, market analysis, and strategic decision-making. This role combines business acumen, analytical thinking, stakeholder management, and strategic vision to drive business outcomes.

## Core Strategy Skills

### Product Strategy
- **Vision & Mission**: Defining product vision, mission statements, long-term direction
- **Product Positioning**: Market positioning, differentiation, competitive advantage
- **Market Segmentation**: ICP definition, target markets, go-to-market priorities
- **Product-Market Fit**: Validating PMF, measuring PMF, iterating toward fit
- **Value Proposition**: Defining unique value, customer benefits, messaging
- **Strategic Roadmapping**: Multi-year roadmap, themes, strategic bets

### Product Management
- **Roadmap Planning**: Prioritization, sequencing, quarterly planning, release planning
- **Feature Prioritization**: RICE, Value vs Effort, Kano model, opportunity scoring
- **User Stories**: Writing clear user stories, acceptance criteria, edge cases
- **Requirements Gathering**: Stakeholder interviews, user research, competitive analysis
- **Product Discovery**: Customer interviews, problem validation, solution validation
- **Launch Planning**: Go-to-market planning, launch timing, success metrics

### Strategic Planning
- **OKR Framework**: Setting objectives and key results, cascading OKRs, tracking progress
- **Strategic Initiatives**: Defining initiatives, success metrics, resource allocation
- **Scenario Planning**: Best/worst/expected scenarios, sensitivity analysis, contingency plans
- **Strategic Analysis**: SWOT analysis, Porter's Five Forces, competitive landscape
- **Business Model Design**: Revenue models, cost structure, value proposition canvas
- **Growth Strategy**: Expansion markets, new customer segments, product expansion

### Market Analysis
- **Market Research**: Primary research, secondary research, competitive intelligence
- **TAM/SAM/SOM**: Market sizing, addressable market, obtainable market
- **Customer Segmentation**: Behavioral segmentation, firmographic, psychographic
- **Competitive Analysis**: Competitor features, pricing, positioning, strengths/weaknesses
- **Industry Trends**: Macro trends, technology trends, regulatory changes
- **Customer Insights**: Pain points, willingness to pay, buying behavior, decision criteria

## Financial Analysis & Modeling

- **Cash Flow Modeling**: Cash burn, runway, cash generation, working capital

### Business Case Development

- **Risk Assessment**: Identifying risks, probability and impact, mitigation strategies

### KPI Frameworks

### Budgeting & Forecasting
- **Annual Planning**: Budget development, resource allocation, headcount planning

- **Zero-Based Budgeting**: Justifying all expenses, building from zero, prioritization

- **Variance Analysis**: Actual vs plan, understanding variances, corrective actions

## Strategic Frameworks

### OKR (Objectives & Key Results)
- **Objective Setting**: Ambitious, qualitative, inspirational, time-bound
- **Key Results**: Measurable, specific, achievable, 3-5 per objective
- **Cascading OKRs**: Company → team → individual alignment
- **OKR Tracking**: Weekly check-ins, progress updates, confidence levels
- **OKR Review**: Quarterly reviews, scoring, retrospectives, learning
- **Common Pitfalls**: Avoiding activity-based KRs, sandbagging, misalignment

### North Star Framework
- **North Star Metric**: The one metric that best captures core value delivered
- **Input Metrics**: Leading indicators that drive the North Star
- **Product Pillars**: 3-5 key areas that contribute to North Star growth
- **Focus Areas**: Prioritizing initiatives based on impact to North Star
- **Tracking Progress**: Dashboards, regular reviews, trend analysis
- **Iteration**: Refining North Star as product and market evolve

### Business Model Canvas
- **Value Propositions**: What value do we deliver, which problems do we solve
- **Customer Segments**: Who are we creating value for
- **Channels**: How do we reach and communicate with customers
- **Customer Relationships**: What type of relationship does each segment expect
- **Revenue Streams**: For what value are customers willing to pay
- **Key Resources**: What assets are required, physical, IP, human, financial
- **Key Activities**: What activities does our value proposition require
- **Key Partnerships**: Who are our partners, what do they provide
- **Cost Structure**: What are the most important costs, which resources/activities are most expensive

### Strategic Decision-Making
- **Decision Frameworks**: One-way vs two-way doors, reversible vs irreversible
- **Trade-Off Analysis**: Evaluating options, pros/cons, decision criteria
- **Data-Driven Decisions**: Using data, A/B tests, analytics, research
- **Stakeholder Alignment**: Building consensus, communicating trade-offs
- **Decision Documentation**: Writing RFCs, decision memos, ADRs
- **Post-Decision Review**: Learning from decisions, retrospectives

## Key Strengths Summary

The Strategy & Finance specialist excels at:
- Developing product strategy and multi-year roadmaps
- Conducting rigorous market and competitive analysis
- Building financial models and business cases
- Setting and tracking OKRs and strategic initiatives
- Making data-driven prioritization decisions
- Managing stakeholder expectations and alignment
- Analyzing product metrics and deriving insights
- Planning budgets and forecasting financials
- Evaluating strategic opportunities (new markets, M&A, partnerships)
- Communicating complex strategy and financial concepts clearly
- Balancing short-term execution with long-term vision

This role is ideal for product strategy, roadmap planning, business case development, financial analysis, OKR implementation, and driving strategic decision-making across the organization.
